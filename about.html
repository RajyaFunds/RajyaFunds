About RajyaFunds

RajyaFunds was founded with a singular mission — to make personal finance simple, accessible, and actionable for every Indian. Whether you are a first-generation investor, a salaried professional, or a young aspirant planning your first major purchase, RajyaFunds provides the tools and insights to help you make confident financial decisions.

At its core, RajyaFunds is built on the belief that financial clarity should not be the privilege of a few. Complex decisions — such as whether to take a home loan today or invest through SIPs and buy later — deserve data-backed, honest answers. That is exactly what this platform aims to deliver.

From intelligent calculators to in-depth articles, every resource on RajyaFunds is designed to empower you with knowledge and confidence on your financial journey.

The Founder's Story

Raj Pandey is a humanities graduate from Jabalpur, Madhya Pradesh, holding a Bachelor's degree in History, Economics & Political Science and a Master's degree in Sociology from Rani Durgavati Vishwavidyalaya (RDVV). His academic foundation gave him a deep understanding of human behaviour, economic systems, and societal structures — knowledge that directly informs how RajyaFunds communicates finance to real people.

Raj spent several years preparing for the UPSC Civil Services Examination — one of India's most demanding competitive exams. That period, though challenging, sharpened his analytical thinking, research discipline, and understanding of India's economic and policy landscape. These qualities remain central to how RajyaFunds approaches financial education.

With a growing interest in technology and entrepreneurship, Raj pivoted toward building something tangible. Despite having no formal coding background, he taught himself to work with modern AI tools to design and develop RajyaFunds entirely on his own — a testament to the belief that determination and the right tools can overcome any technical barrier.

His long-term vision is to build a fintech platform that bridges the gap between financial products and the everyday Indian — making wealth-building not just possible, but straightforward.
